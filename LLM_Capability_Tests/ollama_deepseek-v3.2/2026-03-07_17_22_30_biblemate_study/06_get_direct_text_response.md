Here are the requested Bible verses, providing a foundational scriptural understanding of *Agape* love.

### **1 John 4:7-8 (ESV)**
**7** Beloved, let us love one another, for love is from God, and whoever loves has been born of God and knows God. **8** Anyone who does not love does not know God, because God is love.

**Insight:** This passage establishes the ultimate origin and nature of *agape* love. It is not merely a human emotion but a divine reality that flows from God's very being. Our capacity to love in this selfless way is evidence of a transformed life connected to Him.

### **John 13:34-35 (ESV)**
**34** A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another: just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another. **35** By this all people will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another.

**Insight:** Here, Jesus defines the standard and mission of *agape* love. The command is "new" because its measure is "just as I have loved you"—pointing to His impending sacrificial death. This love is the visible, tangible mark that identifies His followers to the world.

### **1 Corinthians 13:4-7 (ESV)**
**4** Love is patient and kind; love does not envy or boast; it is not arrogant **5** or rude. It does not insist on its own way; it is not irritable or resentful; **6** it does not rejoice at wrongdoing, but rejoices with the truth. **7** Love bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.

**Insight:** This is the classic exposition of *agape* love in action. Paul moves from theory to practice, describing what this God-sourced love looks like in daily human relationships. It is primarily *active* (patient, kind) and *self-giving* (not self-seeking). This passage provides the practical test for what genuine, Spirit-produced love is and is not.

**Together, these passages reveal *Agape* love as:**
1.  **Divine in Origin** (from God's nature).
2.  **Christ-like in Standard** (measured by Jesus' sacrifice).
3.  **Active in Practice** (defined by concrete attitudes and actions).
4.  **Essential for Witness** (the proof of authentic discipleship).
