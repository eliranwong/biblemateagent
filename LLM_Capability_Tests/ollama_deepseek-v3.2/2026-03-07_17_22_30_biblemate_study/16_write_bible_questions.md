Write 3-5 open-ended, thought-provoking group discussion questions based on the biblical passages 1 John 4:7-8, John 13:34-35, and 1 Corinthians 13:4-7. The questions should be designed to stimulate personal reflection and conversation among secondary school students aged 11-16, helping them explore the concept of *Agape* love. Ensure the questions are accessible and relevant to their daily experiences.

Here are 5 open-ended, thought-provoking discussion questions designed for a youth Bible study on *Agape* love, based on the specified passages.

### **Group Discussion Questions**

1.  **The "Why" Behind the Action:** 1 John 4:7 says love "comes from God." Think of a time someone showed you real, selfless kindness, or a time you chose to be kind when you didn't feel like it. How does knowing that God *is* love (v.8) change the way we understand where that kind of action ultimately comes from?

2.  **Love as Your ID Card:** Jesus says in John 13:35 that our love for one another is how people will know we are His disciples. If this kind of love was the *only* identifier for a follower of Jesus, what would people at your school or in your online spaces notice? What would look different from the way everyone else typically acts?

3.  **The 1 Corinthians 13 Challenge:** Read the description in 1 Corinthians 13:4-7. Which one of these traits (e.g., patience, not keeping a record of wrongs, protecting others) do you think is the **most difficult** to show at school or at home? Why do you think that one is so hard, and what would it practically look like to try it this week?

4.  **Feelings vs. Action:** The love described in these passages is presented more as a choice and an action than a feeling. Can you think of a situation where you can **choose to *act* in love** toward someone even if you don't particularly *like* them or *feel* loving toward them at that moment? What might that action be?

5.  **The "New Command" in a Digital World:** Jesus gave the command to "love one another" in person (John 13:34). How can we obey this same command in our digital interactions (group chats, social media comments, sharing posts)? Using the 1 Corinthians 13 description, what would it look like for love to be "patient," "kind," and "not delight in evil" online?
