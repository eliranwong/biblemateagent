Write a short, reflective devotional piece on the topic of *Agape* Love, based on the Bible passages 1 John 4:7-8, John 13:34-35, and 1 Corinthians 13:4-7. The devotion should be tailored for secondary school students (ages 11-16) and designed to help them personally internalize the concept, connecting the biblical teaching to their own thoughts, feelings, and relationship with God. The tone should be engaging and suitable for a youth group or classroom setting, focusing on personal application and reflection.

### **Your Love ID: More Than a Feeling**

Ever scroll through your feed and see #Love for a new game, a song, or a pair of trainers? We use that word for everything we really *like*. But what if I told you the Bible talks about a love that’s less about what you *like* and more about who you *are*? That’s *Agape*.

It starts with a crazy truth from 1 John 4:7-8: **God doesn’t just *have* love or *show* love. God *is* love.** It’s His core identity, like your DNA. So, if you’ve welcomed God into your life, His love-identity is in you. That means *Agape* isn’t a warm, fuzzy emotion that depends on how someone treats you. It’s a superpower you have access to—the power to choose what’s best for someone else, even when it’s difficult.

Jesus makes this our mission. In John 13:34-35, He says, **“Love one another. As I have loved you, so you must love one another.”** How did He love? By washing the feet of the friend who would betray Him. By dying for people who hated Him. His love was an action, a choice. He then says this love is our **ID card** to the world. Not our grades, our style, or our follower count. The way we treat others—especially the unlikable, the left-out, the annoying—is the proof that we’re His.

So, what does this action-hero love look like in Maths class or on the group chat? 1 Corinthians 13:4-7 is our blueprint. It’s less about hearts and more about hands and mouths.

*   **“Love is patient”** is choosing to listen (again) to your sibling’s long story.
*   **“Love is not rude”** is not eye-rolling when a teacher corrects you.
*   **“Love does not delight in evil but rejoices with the truth”** is refusing to forward that embarrassing meme of someone, and maybe even telling the group chat to stop.
*   **“Love always protects”** is the courage to sit with the person eating alone.

The feeling in your stomach when someone annoys you? That’s normal. But the next move you make—that’s where *Agape* kicks in. It’s the choice, powered by God’s Spirit in you, to respond with patience, kindness, and protection instead of irritation, gossip, or payback.

**Your Call to Action: The One-Word Challenge**
This week, take one word from 1 Corinthians 13:4-7. Maybe **“kind”** or **“patient.”** Write it on your hand or set it as your phone lock screen. Your mission is to catch yourself *once a day* before you react. When you’re about to snap at a parent, remember your word. When you’re tempted to ignore a new person, remember your word. Make the choice to act on it just once. That’s you, letting God’s love-ID show.

**Prayer:**
God, you are love. Sometimes love feels impossible, especially when people are difficult. Thank you that your love in me isn’t a weak feeling, but a powerful choice. Today, help me to see one opportunity to put that love into action. Help me to choose patience over annoyance, or kindness over indifference. Let something I do today make my love for others—and your love in me—obvious. Amen.
