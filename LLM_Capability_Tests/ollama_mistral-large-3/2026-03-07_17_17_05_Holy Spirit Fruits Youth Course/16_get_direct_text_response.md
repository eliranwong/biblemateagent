### **Example Lesson: "Love"**
*(Completed to demonstrate the template in action)*

| **Section**               | **Content**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1. Lesson Title**       | **Love**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **2. Lesson Objectives**  | 1. Students will define *agape* love biblically and contrast it with cultural definitions (e.g., romance, tolerance). <br> 2. Students will identify one sacrificial act of love to practice this week. <br> 3. Students will articulate how love fulfills the "law" (Galatians 5:14).                                                                                                                      |
| **3. Duration**           | 60 minutes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **4. Materials Needed**   | - Bibles (NIV/ESV) <br> - Printable "Love in Action" worksheet <br> - YouTube clip: "The Love Chapter – 1 Corinthians 13 (3-min animated video)" <br> - Index cards + pens <br> - Whiteboard + markers <br> - "Compliment Chain" slips (pre-cut paper)                                                                                                                                                     |
| **5. Ice-breaker Activity** | **Name**: *Compliment Chain* <br> **Instructions**: <br> 1. Give each student 3 slips of paper. <br> 2. Ask them to write **one genuine compliment** (not appearance-based) for 3 different peers (e.g., "You’re a great listener"). <br> 3. Collect slips in a bowl, then redistribute randomly. <br> 4. Each student reads their compliment aloud (or silently if shy). <br> **Time**: 10 minutes <br> **Adaptations**: <br> - *Simplified*: Provide sentence starters (e.g., "I appreciate how you..."). <br> - *Extended*: Have students explain *why* they chose their compliments. |
| **6. Bible Teaching**     | **Key Scripture Passages**: <br> - Galatians 5:22–23 <br> - 1 Corinthians 13:4–7 <br> - John 15:13 <br> - 1 John 4:7–8                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
|                           | **Contextual Explanation**: <br> Paul wrote Galatians to counter legalism in the early church (e.g., Gentiles being told they must follow Jewish law to be saved). In Galatians 5:14, he argues that **love fulfills the entire law**—not rules, but a transformed heart. The Greek word for love here is *agape*, a selfless, sacrificial love that seeks the good of others (even enemies). This was radical in a culture where love was often transactional (e.g., patron-client relationships). Today, *agape* challenges our "me-first" culture, where love is often reduced to romance or tolerance. |
|                           | **Modern Application**: <br> UK teens often equate love with **romantic relationships** (e.g., Valentine’s Day, Netflix rom-coms) or **tolerance** ("love is accepting everyone’s choices"). But *agape* love is **active and sacrificial**—it might look like sitting with the lonely kid at lunch, forgiving a friend who gossiped, or helping a sibling with homework when you’d rather game. It’s not about feelings but **choices**. For example, Jesus’ love led Him to die for us *while we were still sinners* (Romans 5:8)—no conditions. How might this redefine how you treat your "enemies" (e.g., bullies, difficult teachers)? |
| **7. Illustrations, Devotions, Applications** | **Illustrations**: <br> - **Story**: Share the story of **Corrie ten Boom**, a Dutch Christian who forgave her Nazi captors after WWII (watch her testimony on YouTube: "Corrie ten Boom on Forgiveness"). <br> - **Multimedia**: Show the 3-minute animated video of 1 Corinthians 13 (link in Materials). Pause at 1:45 to discuss: *"How is this different from how love is shown in movies?"* <br> - **Case Study**: Read aloud a **UK teen’s story** (e.g., "I showed love by befriending a classmate with autism—it was hard, but I saw how it changed them"). |
|                           | **Devotions**: <br> - **Journal Prompt**: *"Write a letter to God thanking Him for loving you unconditionally. Then ask Him to show you one person you can love sacrificially this week."* <br> - **Reflection Question**: *"Who is someone you find hard to love? Why? Pray for them now."* <br> - **Prayer Guide**: *"God, help me to love [name] like You love me. Show me one way to serve them this week."*                                                                 |
|                           | **Applications**: <br> - **Challenge**: *"Perform one act of *agape* love this week that requires sacrifice (e.g., give up your seat on the bus, help a sibling with chores without being asked)."* <br> - **Takeaway**: *"Memorize 1 Corinthians 13:4–5 and recite it to a friend."* <br> - **Creative Response**: *"Design a social media post (or draw a comic strip) showing *agape* love in action at school."*                                                                 |
| **8. Guidance for Helpers** | **Key Discussion Points**: <br> - Emphasize: *"Love is a *fruit* of the Spirit—it grows when we abide in Christ (John 15:5). It’s not about trying harder but relying on God."* <br> - Clarify: *"Avoid equating love with *liking* someone or *tolerating* sin. Love speaks truth (Ephesians 4:15)."* <br> - Highlight: *"Jesus modeled *agape* by washing His disciples’ feet (John 13:1–17)—love is humble service."*                                                                 |
|                           | **Potential Pitfalls**: <br> - *"Don’t let students confuse love with *niceness*—Jesus overturned tables in the temple (John 2:15)!"* <br> - *"Address guilt if students feel they ‘fail’ at love. Remind them: ‘We love because He first loved us’ (1 John 4:19)."* <br> - *"Sensitive topics: Abuse (e.g., ‘Does love mean staying in a toxic relationship?’). Have safeguarding contacts ready."*                                                                 |
|                           | **Adaptations**: <br> - *Simplified*: Use a **"Love Thermometer"** (draw a thermometer on the board; students rate how "hot" or "cold" their love is in different areas). <br> - *Extended*: Debate: *"Is it loving to call out a friend’s sin? Use Scripture to support your answer."* <br> - *Large Groups*: Split into small groups to discuss: *"What’s one way our youth group can show *agape* love to our community?"*                                                                 |
| **9. Group Discussion Questions** | **Understanding**: <br> 1. *"How does 1 Corinthians 13:4–7 redefine ‘love’ compared to how it’s portrayed in social media or movies?"* <br> 2. *"Why does Paul say love is the ‘greatest’ in 1 Corinthians 13:13? How does this connect to Galatians 5:14?"* <br> 3. *"Read John 15:13. How did Jesus demonstrate *agape* love? How can we follow His example?"*                                                                                                                                 |
|                           | **Application**: <br> 4. *"Where in your life is it hardest to show *agape* love? Why?"* <br> 5. *"What’s one *specific* way you can practice *agape* love at school this week? (Be practical!)"* <br> 6. *"How might *agape* love change your response to someone who bullies you or excludes you?"*                                                                                                                                 |
|                           | **Accountability**: <br> 7. *"Share a time you saw someone else demonstrate *agape* love. What did you learn from them?"* <br> 8. *"How can we encourage each other to grow in *agape* love as a youth group?"*                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **10. Assessment**        | **Exit Ticket**: *"Write one word to describe how you’ll show *agape* love this week. Fold your paper and drop it in the ‘Love Box’ as you leave."* <br> **Creative Response**: *"Design a ‘Love in Action’ poster for our youth room (include a verse and a practical example)."* <br> **Pair Share**: *"Tell a partner your ‘action plan’ for *agape* love this week. Pray for each other."*                                                                 |
| **11. Supplementary Resources** | - **Videos**: <br>   - "The Bible Project: Fruit of the Spirit (5-min overview)" [Link] <br>   - "Corrie ten Boom on Forgiveness" (YouTube, 4 mins) <br> - **Books**: <br>   - *"The Love Dare"* by Alex Kendrick (for older teens) <br>   - *"God’s Love"* by Sally Lloyd-Jones (for younger teens) <br> - **Apps**: <br>   - "Bible Lens" (create verse art for 1 Corinthians 13) <br>   - "Echo Prayer" (pray for people you find hard to love) <br> - **Music**: <br>   - "How He Loves" by David Crowder Band <br>   - "Love Won’t Let Me Down" by Hillsong Young & Free <br> - **UK-Specific**: <br>   - Childline’s resources on bullying: [www.childline.org.uk] <br>   - "The Message" UK youth Bible (accessible translation) |
