### **Example Lesson: "Love"**

| **Section**               | **Content**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1. Lesson Title**       | **Love**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **2. Lesson Objectives**  | 1. Students will define *agape* love biblically and contrast it with cultural definitions (e.g., romance, tolerance)<br>2. Students will identify one sacrificial act of love to practice this week<br>3. Students will articulate how love fulfills the "law" (Galatians 5:14)                                                                                                                      |
| **3. Duration**           | 60 minutes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **4. Materials Needed**   | - Bibles (NIV/ESV)<br>- Printable "Love in Action" worksheet<br>- YouTube clip: "The Love Chapter - 1 Corinthians 13 (3-min animated video)"<br>- Index cards + pens<br>- Whiteboard + markers<br>- "Compliment Chain" slips (pre-cut paper)                                                                                                                                                     |
| **5. Ice-breaker Activity** | **Name**: *Compliment Chain*<br>**Instructions**:<br>1. Give each student 3 slips of paper<br>2. Ask them to write one genuine compliment (not appearance-based) for 3 different peers<br>3. Collect slips in a bowl, then redistribute randomly<br>4. Each student reads their compliment aloud (or silently if shy)<br>**Time**: 10 minutes<br>**Adaptations**:<br>- *Simplified*: Provide sentence starters (e.g., "I appreciate how you...")<br>- *Extended*: Have students explain why they chose their compliments<br>- *Large Groups*: Split into smaller teams |
| **6. Bible Teaching**     | **Key Scripture Passages**:<br>- Galatians 5:22-23<br>- 1 Corinthians 13:4-7<br>- John 15:13<br>- 1 John 4:7-8                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
|                           | **Contextual Explanation**:<br>Paul wrote Galatians to counter legalism in the early church. In Galatians 5:14, he argues that love fulfills the entire law - not rules, but a transformed heart. The Greek word for love here is *agape*, a selfless, sacrificial love that seeks the good of others (even enemies). This was radical in a culture where love was often transactional. Today, *agape* challenges our "me-first" culture where love is reduced to romance or tolerance. |
|                           | **Modern Application**:<br>UK teens often equate love with romantic relationships (e.g., Valentine's Day, Netflix rom-coms) or tolerance ("love is accepting everyone's choices"). But *agape* love is active and sacrificial - it might look like sitting with the lonely kid at lunch, forgiving a friend who gossiped, or helping a sibling with homework when you'd rather game. It's not about feelings but choices. For example, Jesus' love led Him to die for us while we were still sinners (Romans 5:8) - no conditions. How might this redefine how you treat your "enemies" (e.g., bullies, difficult teachers)? |
| **7. Illustrations, Devotions, Applications** | **Illustrations**:<br>- Story: Corrie ten Boom forgiving her Nazi captors (YouTube: "Corrie ten Boom on Forgiveness")<br>- Multimedia: 3-minute animated video of 1 Corinthians 13<br>- Case Study: UK teen befriending a classmate with autism                                                                                                                                                          |
|                           | **Devotions**:<br>- Journal prompt: "Write a letter to God thanking Him for loving you unconditionally. Ask Him to show you one person to love sacrificially this week."<br>- Reflection question: "Who is someone you find hard to love? Why? Pray for them now."<br>- Prayer guide: "God, help me to love [name] like You love me. Show me one way to serve them this week."                                                                 |
|                           | **Applications**:<br>- Challenge: "Perform one act of *agape* love this week that requires sacrifice (e.g., give up your seat on the bus, help a sibling with chores without being asked)"<br>- Takeaway: "Memorize 1 Corinthians 13:4-5 and recite it to a friend"<br>- Creative response: "Design a social media post or comic strip showing *agape* love in action at school"                                                                 |
| **8. Guidance for Helpers** | **Key Discussion Points**:<br>- Emphasize: "Love is a fruit of the Spirit - it grows when we abide in Christ (John 15:5)"<br>- Clarify: "Avoid equating love with liking someone or tolerating sin. Love speaks truth (Ephesians 4:15)"<br>- Highlight: "Jesus modeled *agape* by washing His disciples' feet (John 13:1-17) - love is humble service"                                                                 |
|                           | **Potential Pitfalls**:<br>- "Don't let students confuse love with niceness - Jesus overturned tables in the temple (John 2:15)!"<br>- "Address guilt if students feel they 'fail' at love. Remind them: 'We love because He first loved us' (1 John 4:19)"<br>- "Sensitive topics: Abuse (e.g., 'Does love mean staying in a toxic relationship?'). Have safeguarding contacts ready."                                                                 |
|                           | **Adaptations**:<br>- *Simplified*: Use a "Love Thermometer" (draw a thermometer; students rate how "hot" or "cold" their love is in different areas)<br>- *Extended*: Debate: "Is it loving to call out a friend's sin? Use Scripture to support your answer"<br>- *Large Groups*: Split into small groups to discuss: "What's one way our youth group can show *agape* love to our community?"                                                                 |
| **9. Group Discussion Questions** | **Understanding**:<br>1. How does 1 Corinthians 13:4-7 redefine 'love' compared to how it's portrayed in social media or movies?<br>2. Why does Paul say love is the "greatest" in 1 Corinthians 13:13? How does this connect to Galatians 5:14?<br>3. Read John 15:13. How did Jesus demonstrate *agape* love? How can we follow His example?                                                                 |
|                           | **Application**:<br>4. Where in your life is it hardest to show *agape* love? Why?<br>5. What's one specific way you can practice *agape* love at school this week?<br>6. How might *agape* love change your response to someone who bullies you or excludes you?                                                                                                                                              |
|                           | **Accountability**:<br>7. Share a time you saw someone else demonstrate *agape* love. What did you learn from them?<br>8. How can we encourage each other to grow in *agape* love as a youth group?                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **10. Assessment**        | Exit Ticket: "Write one word to describe how you'll show *agape* love this week. Fold your paper and drop it in the 'Love Box' as you leave."<br>Creative Response: "Design a 'Love in Action' poster for our youth room (include a verse and practical example)"<br>Pair Share: "Tell a partner your 'action plan' for *agape* love this week. Pray for each other."                                                                 |
| **11. Supplementary Resources** | - **Videos**: "The Bible Project: Fruit of the Spirit" (5-min overview)<br>- **Books**: "The Love Dare" by Alex Kendrick (older teens), "God's Love" by Sally Lloyd-Jones (younger teens)<br>- **Apps**: Bible Lens (create verse art), Echo Prayer (pray for people you find hard to love)<br>- **Music**: "How He Loves" by David Crowder Band, "Love Won't Let Me Down" by Hillsong Young & Free<br>- **UK-Specific**: Childline's resources on bullying, "The Message" UK youth Bible |
