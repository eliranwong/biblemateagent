- **Comprehension questions:** Distinguish makrothumia (long-temperedness, endurance under provocation, delayed gratification) from short-fuse reactions, passive resignation, or laziness; explain the farmer metaphor in James 5:7 and the race metaphor in Hebrews 12:1-3; contrast God's patience (2 Peter 3:9) with human impatience

**Week 5 (Kindness/Chrestotes):**
- **Scriptures:** Ephesians 4:32; Colossians 3:12; Luke 6:35; Ruth 2:8-16; 2 Samuel 9:1-13; Titus 3:4
- **Comprehension questions:** Distinguish chrestotes (moral excellence, Christ-like kindness, benevolent action) from mere niceness or people-pleasing; explain Boaz's kindness to Ruth (Ruth 2:8-16) and David's kindness to Mephibosheth (2 Samuel 9) as undeserved benevolence; contrast God's kindness to the ungrateful (Luke 6:35, Titus 3:4) with transactional friendship

**Week 6 (Goodness/Agathosune):**
- **Scriptures:** Galatians 6:9-10; Ephesians 2:10; Titus 3:14; Acts 10:38; Ruth 2-4; Genesis 39:4-6
- **Comprehension questions:** Distinguish agathosune (proactive beneficence, active doing of good) from passive avoidance of evil or mere neutrality; explain Jesus "going about doing good" (Acts 10:38) and Joseph's goodness in Potiphar's house (Genesis 39:4-6) as active integrity; contrast "doing good to all" (Galatians 6:10) with selective charity
- **Application questions:** Address exam cheating dilemmas and academic integrity under peer pressure, community volunteering in post-COVID British context (food banks, elderly care), ethical decision-making regarding "the other" (refugees, marginalized groups, asylum seekers in UK context), and proactive doing of good vs. private morality
- **Controversial scenarios:** "Is doing good to refugees unfair to British citizens who are struggling?" "Should you do good by reporting a friend for cheating or is that betrayal?" "Is it good to give money to homeless people or does that enable addiction?"
- **Differentiation:** Concrete thinkers (Years 7-8) focus on practical decisions about copying homework, helping struggling classmates, picking up litter, welcoming new students; Abstract thinkers (Years 10-11) develop ethical frameworks for "doing good" in pluralistic society, agathosune as proactive justice vs. private morality, the politics of goodness in Brexit Britain regarding immigration and asylum policy

Ensure all questions maintain the theological framework that these are Spirit-produced fruits (Galatians 5:22-23), not self-generated achievements, and address UK-specific contexts including multiculturalism, digital citizenship (Online Safety Act 2023), academic pressure, and post-COVID community fragmentation.

**WEEK 4: MAKROTHUMIA (μακροθυμία) — LONG-TEMPERED ENDURANCE**
*Scriptures: James 5:7-11; 2 Peter 3:9; Romans 15:4-5; Hebrews 12:1-3; Luke 8:15*

---

**CONCRETE THINKERS (YEARS 7-8)**

**Comprehension Questions**
1. James 5:7 says a farmer waits for rain. What can the farmer actually do to make the crops grow faster? What does this teach us about waiting for exam results or CAMHS appointments?
2. Hebrews 12:1 talks about "running with endurance." Is this a short sprint or a long race? How is GCSE revision more like a marathon than a 100-metre dash?
3. In Luke 8:15, the good soil "produces a crop with patience." What happens if you dig up seeds every day to check if they're growing?
4. 2 Peter 3:9 says God is "patient" and doesn't want anyone to perish. Does this mean God is slow, or that He is being kind by waiting?

**Application Questions**
5. You are refreshing your email every two minutes waiting for mock results. What would James 5:7-8 say about this? What could you do instead of checking your phone?
6. You have been on the NHS CAMHS waiting list for eight months. How does knowing God is also "waiting" with you (Romans 15:5) help when you feel forgotten?
7. You are tempted to check TikTok instead of finishing your Maths homework. How is this "short-fuse" thinking? What does "long-temperedness" look like here?
8. Your friend is annoying you during a group project and you want to ghost them. How is Hebrews 12:1-3 about enduring difficulty without giving up?

**Controversial Scenarios**
9. **The Laziness Question:** Your classmate says they are "being patient" by not revising yet, waiting for "inspiration." Is this makrothumia or just poor time management? Where is the line between Spirit-produced patience and lazy procrastination?
10. **The Passive Waiting Problem:** Your family is struggling financially and your mum says "we just need to wait for God to provide." Should you wait passively for God to change things, or should you actively apply for benefits/job? Does Romans 15:5 mean we do nothing?
11. **The Disrespectful Friendship:** Your best friend keeps cancelling plans last minute and disrespecting you. You've been "patient" for six months. Is this makrothumia (long-temperedness) or are you being a doormat? When does patience become foolishness?

**WEEK 5: CHRESTOTES (χρηστότης) — CHRIST-LIKE MORAL EXCELLENCE**
*Scriptures: Ephesians 4:32; Colossians 3:12; Luke 6:35; Ruth 2:8-16; 2 Samuel 9:1-13; Titus 3:4*

---

**CONCRETE THINKERS (YEARS 7-8)**

**Comprehension Questions**
1. Ephesians 4:32 says "be kind and compassionate." Colossians 3:12 says "clothe yourselves with kindness." Is kindness something you put on like a uniform, or something you feel inside?
2. In Ruth 2:8-16, Boaz notices Ruth is a foreigner and poor. What specific actions does he take to help her? Does he help because she can pay him back?
3. 2 Samuel 9 says David searched for Mephibosheth to show him "God's kindness." Why did David help someone from the family of his enemy Saul?
4. Luke 6:35 says God is kind to "ungrateful and wicked people." Does this mean God rewards bad behaviour? What is the difference between kindness and rewarding sin?

**Application Questions**
5. You see a Year 7 student drop their books in the corridor and everyone laughs. What would Boaz do in this situation? What specific action could you take?
6. Someone in your form group is unpopular and has no one to sit with at lunch. How is inviting them to your table like David inviting Mephibosheth to the king's table?
7. A WhatsApp group is sharing embarrassing photos of a classmate. Colossians 3:12 says you are "clothed in kindness." What does kindness look like in this digital space?
8. Your friend is being cyberbullied. Is it kind to just "like" their post to show support, or do you need to do something more active like Boaz did for Ruth?

**Controversial Scenarios**
9. **The Bullying Kindness:** A student keeps punching you in the arm and calling you names. Your youth leader says "be kind to your enemies." Should you keep being nice to them, or is this enabling abuse? Where is the line between Luke 6:35 and self-protection?
10. **The Hard Truth:** Your friend is failing Maths because they're always on their phone. Is it kind to tell them the truth (which will hurt) or kind to say nothing (which lets them fail)? Does chrestotes mean always being nice?
11. **The Resource Question:** Your school is collecting money for refugees. Some students say "we should help British kids first." Is it kind to help outsiders when local people are struggling? Does David helping Mephibosheth mean he didn't help his own soldiers?

**ABSTRACT THINKERS (YEARS 10-11)**

**Comprehension Questions**
12. Distinguish between *chrestotes* (moral excellence, benevolent action) and *philophronesis* (people-pleasing) using Ephesians 4:32. How does kindness rooted in divine forgiveness differ from niceness seeking approval?
13. Analyze Boaz's kindness (Ruth 2:8-16) through the lens of power dynamics. How does his position as landowner inform his obligation to the marginalized Moabite widow? What does this teach about privilege and responsibility?
14. 2 Samuel 9:1-13 demonstrates covenant faithfulness (*chesed*) translated as *chrestotes*. How does David's restoration of Mephibosheth challenge the "cancel culture" logic of permanent exclusion for ancestral sin?
15. Luke 6:35 commands loving enemies and lending without expecting repayment. How does this subvert the transactional nature of social capital in British secondary schools?

**Application Questions**
16. Critique "cancel culture" through the lens of 2 Samuel 9. If David discovered that Mephibosheth had made offensive comments years ago, would he still have restored him? How does *chrestotes* navigate justice and mercy when someone has caused harm?
17. In multicultural Britain, how does *chrestotes* as "moral excellence" differ from cultural relativism? When a cultural practice conflicts with biblical kindness (e.g., honour-based violence), how do you practice cross-cultural benevolence without moral compromise?
18. Evaluate the Online Safety Act 2023's requirement to prevent cyberbullying against Luke 6:35's command to kindness toward the "ungrateful." Can you be kind to someone who has trolled you online without enabling their behaviour?
19. How does Titus 3:4's "kindness and love of God our Savior" reframe your approach to students who actively mock your faith? Does *chrestotes* require friendship with those who despise Christianity?

**Controversial Scenarios**
20. **The Cancel Culture Conundrum:** A celebrity made racist comments five years ago and is being "cancelled." They have apologised but lost everything. Is it *chrestotes* to advocate for their restoration (like Mephibosheth) or is it minimizing harm to victims? Where is the line between kindness and enabling?
21. **The Truth vs. Niceness Tension:** Your friend is considering cheating on their UCAS personal statement. Ephesians 4:15 says "speak the truth in love." Is it kinder to tell them the truth (risking the friendship) or to stay silent (preserving harmony)? Does *chrestotes* always feel nice?
22. **The Asylum Debate:** The UK government policy is to deport asylum seekers to Rwanda. Titus 3:4 says God's kindness appeared to us when we were enemies. Is it *chrestotes* to oppose this policy, or is it unkind to British citizens who need housing? How do you navigate political kindness?

**WEEK 6: AGATHOSUNE (ἀγαθωσύνη) — PROACTIVE BENEFICENCE**
*Scriptures: Galatians 6:9-10; Ephesians 2:10; Titus 3:14; Acts 10:38; Ruth 2-4; Genesis 39:4-6*

---

**CONCRETE THINKERS (YEARS 7-8)**

**Comprehension Questions**
1. Acts 10:38 says Jesus "went around doing good." Was Jesus just avoiding bad things, or was He actively looking for good to do? What is the difference?
2. In Genesis 39:4-6, Joseph worked hard even though he was a slave and no one was watching. Why did he do this? Was he trying to get a promotion, or was it goodness?
3. Galatians 6:10 says "do good to all people, especially to the family of believers." Does this mean we only help Christians, or that we help everyone but Christians first?
4. Ephesians 2:10 says we are "created to do good works." Are good works what save us, or what we do because we are saved?

**Application Questions**
5. You see litter in the corridor. Picking it up is "doing good" (agathosune). Walking past is "not doing bad." What would Jesus do (Acts 10:38)?
6. A new student from Syria arrives who doesn't speak English. They look confused about the timetable. How is helping them "doing good" like Boaz helped Ruth?
7. Your friends want to copy your homework. Joseph refused to sin even when no one would know (Genesis 39). Is refusing to let them copy "doing good" or just "not doing bad"?
8. Your school is collecting for the food bank. Is giving one tin "doing good," or does agathosune mean doing more?

**Controversial Scenarios**
9. **The Refugee Question:** Your town has taken in asylum seekers. Some local families are struggling with housing. Is it "doing good" to help the refugees when British citizens need help too? Does Galatians 6:10 mean "especially believers" or "everyone including believers"?
10. **The Cheating Dilemma:** Your best friend is failing and asks to copy your coursework. If you say no, they might fail their GCSE. Is it "good" to help them cheat (so they pass) or "good" to refuse (so they learn integrity)? What does Joseph in Potiphar's house teach us?
11. **The Homeless Debate:** You see a homeless person outside Tesco. Your dad says "don't give them money, they'll spend it on drugs." Is it "doing good" to give money anyway, or is it foolish? Does agathosune mean ignoring consequences?

How does Jesus "doing good" to those who didn't convert (Luke 6:35) inform this?
