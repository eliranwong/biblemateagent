# Title

Holy Spirit Fruits Youth Course

# Original Request



Prepare a comprehensive Youth Bible course (age-range: secondary school students; context: UK) about the fruits of the Holy Spirit. A total of 9 lessons, with each containing:

* ice-breakers

* detailed bible teaching with quoted bible verses

* illustrations, devotions and applications

* guidance for youth team helpers

* group discussion questions

# Refined Request

Act as an expert Youth Ministry Curriculum Developer specializing in UK secondary education contexts. Create a comprehensive 9-week Bible study course titled "Fruits of the Spirit" designed for students aged 11-16.

**Course Specifications:**
1.  **Structure:** 9 lessons, each dedicated to one of the 9 Fruits of the Holy Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23).
2.  **Duration:** Each lesson plan must be designed for a 60-minute session.
3.  **Language & Translation:** Use British English spelling and terminology. Quote scripture from the NIV UK or NLT version for accessibility.
4.  **Tone:** Engaging, relatable, and age-appropriate, avoiding overly theological jargon without explanation.

**Lesson Components:**
For each of the 9 lessons, include the following sections with clear headings:
*   **Learning Objective:** A single sentence stating what students will understand or do by the end.
*   **Ice-Breaker (10 mins):** A fun, low-prep activity to introduce the theme.
*   **Bible Teaching (20 mins):** Detailed exposition with key verses, contextual background, and relatable illustrations/stories.
*   **Application & Devotion (15 mins):** Practical steps for daily life and a short reflective devotion.
*   **Group Discussion (10 mins):** 3-5 open-ended questions to encourage participation.
*   **Helper Guidance (5 mins):** Specific instructions for youth leaders, including safeguarding considerations, preparation tips, and how to handle sensitive discussions.

**Output Format:**
Present the full course in structured Markdown. Use bolding for key terms and bullet points for readability. Ensure cultural references are relevant to UK teenagers (e.g., school life, local culture).
