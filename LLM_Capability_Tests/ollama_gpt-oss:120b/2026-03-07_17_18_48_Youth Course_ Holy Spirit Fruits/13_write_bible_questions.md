## Discussion Questions for a UK Secondary-School Youth Bible Study  
*Each set of questions is linked to the fruit-of-the-Spirit teaching you’ll be using for that lesson.  They are open-ended, culturally-relevant, and easy for a youth leader to prompt.*

---

### 1. **John 13:34-35 – Love**  
1. **What does “love one another as I have loved you” look like in a school hallway where cliques often form?**  
2. **When you see a classmate being excluded, what practical step can you take that would show the kind of love Jesus commands?**  
3. **How can you tell the difference between “liking” someone and the self-sacrificial love the verse describes?**  
4. **In what ways does showing love to others make your own faith more visible to people outside the church?**  
5. **Think of a time you felt loved by a friend or teacher; how can that experience shape the way you love others now?**

---

### 2. **Philippians 4:4 – Joy**  
1. **What are three everyday moments at school (e.g., a break, a lesson, a sports practice) where you could choose to “rejoice in the Lord” instead of focusing on stress?**  
2. **How does joy that comes from God differ from the “happiness” you get from likes, new phones, or good grades?**  
3. **Share a recent situation where you felt joy despite a setback—what helped you keep that joy alive?**  
4. **If a friend is feeling down, what simple, God-centered encouragement could you give them to spark joy?**  
5. **How can you make “rejoicing” a habit rather than a one-off reaction during exam season?**

---

### 3. **John 14:27 – Peace**  
1. **What does “peace that the world cannot give” sound like in the noise of a busy school day or a heated online chat?**  
2. **When anxiety about results or friendships rises, what practical steps help you receive Jesus’ peace instead of panic?**  
3. **How can you be a “peace-bringer” in a group project where opinions clash?**  
4. **Think of a time you felt God’s peace after a stressful event—what reminded you of that promise?**  
5. **In what ways can you model calm confidence for younger siblings or peers who look up to you?**

---

### 4. **James 5:7-8 – Patience**  
1. **What are some “instant-gratification” habits (e.g., scrolling, quick replies) that you need to practice patience with, and how might that change your relationships?**  
2. **How does the farmer’s waiting for a harvest help you understand waiting for things like university offers or friendship repairs?**  
3. **When you feel your heart “strengthening” through patience, what does that feel like physically or emotionally?**  
4. **Share a story of a time you chose patience over a quick reaction—what was the outcome?**  
5. **How can you encourage a friend who is impatient about a goal they’re working toward?**

---

### 5. **Ephesians 4:32 – Kindness**  
1. **What small, everyday actions at school (e.g., sharing notes, offering a seat) demonstrate the “kindness” Paul describes?**  
2. **How does genuine compassion differ from simply being “nice” to avoid conflict?**  
3. **When someone hurts you, what does “forgiving one another as God forgave you” look like in practice?**  
4. **How can you use social media to spread kindness rather than gossip or criticism?**  
5. **Think of a time you received unexpected kindness—how did it affect your attitude for the rest of the day?**

---

### 6. **Psalm 23:6 – Goodness**  
1. **What does it mean for God’s “goodness” to “pursue” you through a typical school week?**  
2. **How can you reflect that goodness in situations where you could easily cheat, lie, or cut corners?**  
3. **When you notice a friend acting with integrity, how can you celebrate that goodness together?**  
4. **What practical steps help you stay aware of God’s goodness when you feel overwhelmed by homework or exams?**  
5. **How does the promise of God’s faithfulness (paired with goodness) give you confidence in future plans, like choosing a career or university?**

---

### 7. **1 Corinthians 4:2 – Faithfulness**  
1. **What does being a “faithful steward” look like when you’re responsible for a group assignment or a sports team?**  
2. **How can you keep promises to friends when peer pressure makes it tempting to change plans?**  
3. **What habits (e.g., a planner, reminders) help you stay reliable in school and at home?**  
4. **Share a moment when someone’s faithfulness inspired you—what did you learn from their example?**  
5. **How does trusting God’s faithfulness encourage you to be faithful to Him in everyday choices?**

---

### 8. **Matthew 11:29 – Gentleness**  
1. **What does “gentle and humble in heart” sound like in a heated debate on a classroom forum or in a sports locker room?**  
2. **When you feel the urge to “win” an argument, how can you choose a gentle response instead?**  
3. **How can you use the image of a “yoke” to describe teamwork in a school project or band practice?**  
4. **Think of a time you felt “rest for your soul” after someone treated you gently—what changed for you?**  
5. **In what ways can you model gentleness to younger siblings or new students who feel nervous at school?**

---

### 9. **2 Timothy 1:7 – Self-Control**  
1. **What are the biggest “spirits of fear” you face at school (e.g., fear of failure, peer rejection), and how can you replace them with God-given power?**  
2. **How does self-control help you manage digital temptations like endless TikTok scrolling during homework?**  
3. **When you feel strong emotions (anger, jealousy), what practical steps can you take to exercise self-control before responding?**  
4. **Share a personal victory where you chose self-control over an impulsive reaction—what did you learn?**  
5. **How can you encourage a friend who struggles with self-control to rely on God’s power and love instead of fear?**

---

These questions are designed to flow naturally from the exegetical teaching you’ll present, prompting personal reflection, peer sharing, and practical application for UK secondary-school students. Feel free to adapt wording or add local examples (e.g., specific school events, popular apps) to make the conversation even more relatable. Happy facilitating!
