### Discussion Questions: The Fruit of Love

**Galatians 5:22 & 1 Corinthians 13:4-7**

#### Exploring the Nature of Love

1.  **Galatians 5:22 lists "love" as the first fruit of the Spirit.** Why do you think Paul lists it first? Do you see it as the source of the other fruits (joy, peace, patience, etc.), or just one item on a list? Explain your reasoning.

2.  **In 1 Corinthians 13:4-7, Paul defines love not by what it feels, but by what it does.** He uses action words like "is patient," "is kind," "keeps no record of wrongs." How does this definition challenge the way love is typically portrayed in movies, songs, or on social media?

3.  **The Greek word used here is *agape* (unconditional, self-sacrificial love).** How is this different from *philia* (friendship love) or *eros* (romantic love)? Why is it significant that the Fruit of the Spirit is specifically *agape* and not just a general feeling of affection?

#### Digging into the Text (1 Corinthians 13:4-7)

4.  **Verse 5 says love "keeps no record of wrongs."** In a school context, this could be translated as "doesn't hold grudges" or "doesn't screenshot mistakes to use against someone later." Why is it so difficult to let go of past hurts? What happens to a friendship when we keep a mental "record" of the other person's failures?

5.  **Verse 4 states that love "is not boastful" and "is not proud."** In a culture that often encourages us to "flex" (show off) our achievements, grades, or possessions, what does it look like to love others without needing to prove we are better than them? How does pride kill love?

6.  **Verse 7 says love "always protects, always trusts, always hopes, always perseveres."** This sounds exhausting! Does this mean we have to be gullible or let people walk all over us? How do you reconcile "always trusting" with healthy boundaries in a relationship?

#### Application in a UK Secondary School Context

7.  **"Banter" is a big part of school culture.** Sometimes banter is fun, but sometimes it crosses the line into hurtfulness. Based on 1 Corinthians 13:5 ("it is not rude, it is not self-seeking"), how can you tell the difference between healthy joking and unloving behavior? How do you handle it when a joke goes too far without ruining the friendship?

8.  **Social Media Application:** Verse 4 says love "is not envious." Instagram and TikTok are platforms designed to make us compare our lives to others' "highlight reels." How does envy affect our ability to love our friends? What practical steps can we take to practice love while scrolling?

9.  **The "Difficult Person" Test:** Verse 4 says love "is not easily angered." Think about the most annoying person in your year group or form class. Is it possible to love (*agape*) someone you don't actually like? If love is an action and not just a feeling, what is one practical thing you could do for that person this week that demonstrates love, even if you don't feel like it?

10. **The Source of Love:** If love is a "fruit" that grows from the Spirit (Galatians 5:22), what does that imply about our ability to manufacture it on our own? How does understanding that God loves *us* unconditionally (Romans 5:8) change our capacity to love others who might not "deserve" it?
